Understanding Drug Addiction

Drug addiction is a complex but treatable disease that affects brain function and behavior.

Substance abuse leads to dependency, making it difficult for individuals to quit without help. It affects decision-making, self-control, and judgment, often leading to severe social and personal consequences.

Drugs manipulate the brain's reward system, releasing excessive dopamine. This makes users crave more, leading to addiction. Over time, higher doses are needed to feel the same effect, worsening dependency.

Common risk factors for addiction include genetic predisposition, peer pressure, stress, and mental health disorders like depression and anxiety.

Rehabilitation programs, therapy, and family support play crucial roles in recovery. Education and awareness are the first steps in preventing addiction and helping those in need.
